Step 1 of 3: Assemblage
Your Starting Point

What multiple, even contradictory, functions does this serve right now?

Different roles thoughts can play:
Protector (keeps me safe) • Motivator (pushes me to try harder) • Organizer (makes sense of chaos) • Connector (links me to others) • Controller (gives me agency)
What else wants to be thought about this? What's emerging as you write?
Notice: How does it feel to think about your thought serving multiple purposes rather than just being "true" or "false"?
Step 2 of 3: Multiplicities

How can this be both true and not true at the same time?

Consider different contexts:
True in the past, less true now • True at work, false at home • True from one perspective, false from another • True sometimes, false other times
What's shifting in your understanding as you explore these contradictions?
Notice: How does it feel to hold contradictory truths about the same thought? What happens in your body when you do this?
Step 3 of 3: Lines of Flight

Given all this complexity, what new possibilities become available?

New pathways might include:
Different questions to ask • New actions to try • Ways to hold the thought more lightly • Experiments to run • Conversations to have
What would you be curious to explore if this thought weren't fixed in place?
Notice: What's different about how you're relating to the original thought now versus when you started?
